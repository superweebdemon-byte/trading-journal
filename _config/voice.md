# Voice & Tone

## Core Principle
Direct and data-first, but human. Respect the trader's time — no fluff, no corporate UX copy. When the moment calls for it, acknowledge the grind.

## Guidelines

**Default tone: Trader-direct**
- Lead with data, not decoration
- "No trades" not "Looks like you haven't imported any trades yet!"
- "Import failed: 3 rows skipped" not "Oops! Something went wrong with your import"
- Button labels are verbs: "Import", "Filter", "Export" — not "Get Started" or "Let's Go"

**When to show warmth:**
- Streaks and milestones — "5-win streak. Discipline pays." (not a paragraph, just a nod)
- Recovery from drawdown — acknowledge it without being patronizing
- First import — brief, encouraging, then get out of the way
- Empty states — give direction, not cheerfulness

**Never do:**
- Exclamation marks in error messages
- Emoji in data contexts
- "Oops", "Uh oh", or any cutesy error language
- Motivational quotes or trading platitudes
- Assume the user's emotional state

## Terminology
- "Session" not "trading day" (maps to TradeDay concept)
- "P&L" not "profit and loss" (traders know the abbreviation)
- "Win rate" not "success rate"
- "Drawdown" not "loss period"
- "Entry/Exit" not "open/close" (avoids confusion with market open/close)
- Use contract shorthand: "MNQ", "MYM" — not full names in data views
